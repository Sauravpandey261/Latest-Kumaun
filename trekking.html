Pindari glacier

The Pindari Glacier in Danpur area of Bageshwar district in the Kumaon division is situated at an altitude ranging from 3600 to 5000 meters, approximately 30 kilometers northeast of the Nanda Devi and Nandakot peaks. The Pindari Glacier, which spans a length of several kilometers and has a width ranging from 300 to 400 meters, is a significant glacier in the state. It is particularly popular with adventure enthusiasts who engage in activities like mountaineering and trekking. Tourists are attracted to the Pindari Glacier due to its notably straightforward route in comparison to other glacier itineraries. This glacier originates from the Charan province inside the Nandakot mountain range. It increases in multiple tiers and reaches a final elevation of 3627 meters.
Along with mountaineering and trekking, Pindari is also recognized as a pilgrimage. Pindar, Vishnuganga, Saraswati, Kamath, Baindhya and Bodhini rivers flow in this region. Every year during the favorable weather in May-June and September-October, innumerable tourists, visitors, researchers, trekkers, NCC cadets and mountaineering teams come here, but due to lack of environmental awareness, rare herbs, forest wealth, wildlife and the environment as a whole are greatly harmed by them. Now the environmental balance here has started becoming unstable. According to the latest surveys, in the last 40 years, this glacier has moved down half a kilometer from its original place. Pindari can be easily reached by reaching Song by road, 40 km from Bageshwar, and completing the 45 km journey by trekking. The Pindari route has been made convenient and safe from the tourism point of view since the British rule.

Milam

The Milam Glacier The Gori Ganga originates from a glacier that stretches for 16 kilometers and is situated south of Trishuli Mountain in Pithoragarh district. By amalgamating numerous small and large glaciers, it assumes a colossal size. In several locations, the ice has a thickness exceeding 100 meters. The Milam River, a tributary of the Pindar River, also has its source at this location. The hiking trail to Milam Glacier starts at Munsiyari. Milam Glacier may be accessed by traveling a distance of 64 kilometers through the settlements of Bagudiyar, Maratoli, and Milam.
